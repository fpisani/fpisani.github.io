About

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Institute of Computing of the University of Campinas (UNICAMP), where I am working on an Industrial IoT project under the supervision of Prof. Juliana Freitag Borin. I was a postdoctoral researcher at the Department of Informatics of the Pontifical Catholic University of Rio de Janeiro (PUC-Rio) from 2019 to 2020, where I worked as a part the “Future Internet for Smart Cities” FAPESP project under the supervision of Prof. Markus Endler.

I received a Ph.D. in Computer Science from UNICAMP in 2019, for which I was advised by Prof. Edson Borin. My dissertation is related to the topics of Internet of Things (IoT), resource-constrained devices, and fog computing. I received a B.Sc. degree with honors in Computer Science from UNICAMP in 2014.

My current research interests include high-performance computing, parallel computing, fog computing, constrained devices, Internet of Things, smart cities, Computer Science education, computational thinking, and the use of technology in education.
